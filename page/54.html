Brigadier Sir Nils Olav

2016-10-11

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Nils Olav)
The Norwegian King's Guard performed at the Royal Edinburgh Military Tattoo in 1961. A Lieutenant of the Guard, Nils Egelien was interested in the Zoo's penguin colony. The first king penguin of which was gifted to the Zoo by Norway in 1913. The Guard returned to Edinburgh in 1972 and adopted a penguin. The penguin was named in honour of the then current King Olav V of Norway and Lieutenant Nils Egelien. As King penguins have a typical lifespan of 15-20 years in the wild and up to 30 years in a Zoo, the current mascot is not the original Nils Olav. There have been three so far, the incumbent bird, Nils Olav III took up the role in 2008. The penguin of honour has been promoted several times, the most recent of which was on the 22nd August 2016, when he was given the rank of Brigadier. Edinburgh Zoo was gifted a 4ft-high (1.2m) bronze statue of Nils Olav in 2005, which stands outside the penguin enclosure.

The Silence of Big Ben

2016-10-11

Fact Source(s):
  • NBC News
Big Ben, the largest bell in the Palace of Westminster Bell Tower, officially renamed 'Elizabeth Tower' in 2012 in honour of Queen Elizabeth II on her Diamond Jubilee; is rarely silenced. Its silence usually signifies national mourning, for instance on both Sir Winston Churchill and Margaret Thatcher's funerals; both of whom were Former Prime Ministers. The bell would also occasionally break down or be stopped for maintenance.

Most Times as Olympic Host

2016-10-11

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (List of Olympic Games host cities)
The United Kingdom has hosted three of the Modern Summer Olympic Games. All hosted in the capital, London, during the years: 1908, 1948 and 2012. London was due to host the 1944 Games, but due to the continuation of the Second World War, given the 1948 Games instead. Greece hosted the only Intercalated Games in 1906, but the International Olympic Committee doesn't recognise it as an official Games and thus was not factored into this fact. Athens also hosted the 1896 and 2004 Olympic Games.
