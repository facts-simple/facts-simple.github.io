Third party and fire

15th January, 2017 Interesting

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Fire department)
After the Great Fire of London, insurance companies realised they could earn more from policyholders if they protected the properties from fire and flame themselves. In the following year many private fire brigades were founded, each with a unique fire mark, this durable metal plaque would be clearly displayed on the exterior of the building. When multiple firefighters attended the blaze, the company with the matching plaque would extinguish the flames. If they turned up and no plaque was to be seen, the brigade would leave.

Tricolour treats

15th January, 2017 Food and Drink

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (M&M's)
The son of the founder of the Mars company, was inspired to make M&M's after spotting Spanish soldiers eating Smarties (a similar British chocolate snack) during the Spanish Civil War. The advantage of chocolate covered in a hard sugar shell allowed it to be marketed to the military as an appropriate and long-lasting addition to military rations. A lucrative scheme as America entered the war.

Secret promises

14th January, 2017 History

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Zimmermann Telegram)
As soon as war with the United States was certain, Mexico was to declare war on America with Germany, In return, when America had been defeated, Mexico would be given back control of the Southern territories: Texas, New Mexico and Arizona. After the transatlantic undersea telegraph line was cut at the beginning of the war, Germany had to send its communications via neutral countries, including the American Embassy in London and the Continental United States. Britain secretly intercepted all these communications, including those between Germany and Mexico. This fact was a sensitive one, as Britain was effectively spying on US communications. British Intelligence felt that the contents of the telegrams would forgive the means by which they were acquired. In order to prove its authenticity, a copy was acquired and deciphered in front of the American leaders. To comfort the American public and conceal the British spying effort on the Americans, it was claimed that the interception was the work of the American Secret Service.
