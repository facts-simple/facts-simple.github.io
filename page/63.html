How Cumberbatch played the violin

2016-12-02

Fact Source(s):
  • The Guardian
September is Classical Music Month. The professional violinist was Eos Counsell, a member of the Bond string quartet and a holder of a Honours Degree from the Royal College of Music. Although credit where it's due to Cumberbatch, he was coached how to play the violin like an expert, or at least, appear to on camera by Counsell. He was coached for a few weeks to perfect the movement of the bow, stance and hold.

Evelyn Glennie

2016-12-02

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Evelyn Glennie)
September is Classical Music Month. Dame Evelyn Glennie uses her feet and other parts of her body to 'feel' the music rather than hear it. Her deafness has not hampered her musical career in the slightest, receiving a laureate of the Polar Music Prize in 2015.

Cannon: fortissimo

2016-11-24

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (1812 Overture)
Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky composed the 'The Year 1812' (Its official name) to commemorate the Russian defence against Napoleon's failed invasion of Russia in 1812. The piece contains fairly obvious symbolism, Russian folk songs play 'against' the Marseillaise, the French National Anthem, the turning point of the invasion at the Battle of Borodino is symbolised by Russian cannon fire, countering the National Anthem.
