Deepwater Horizon oil spill

2016-10-12

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Deepwater Horizon oil spill)
​On the 20th of April 2010 the Deepwater Horizon rig suffered an explosion, causing the rig to sink, and take with it the lives of eleven workers, whose bodies have never been recovered. Oil flowed from the sea floor for 87 days until the 5th of July 2010, when the fissure was finally plugged up. The spill area is the habitat for 8,332 species, many of which suffered from birth defects and severe chemical imbalances due to the spill and subsequent clean up. BP was found primarily responsible by way of gross negligence and reckless conduct, the corporation was fined $18.7 billion USD (£12 billion (July 2015)).
