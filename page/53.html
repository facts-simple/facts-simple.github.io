Talk Down Trope

2016-10-11

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Talk down aircraft landing)
This procedure is known as a Talk Down. This has never happened due to several redundancies within Aviation Safety such as regular medical check-ups for pilots, two different meals, and on some long haul flights, a third pilot. There was an occasion in which the pilot of a Boeing 737 had a heart attack, but his co-pilot was still able to land the plane. The co-pilot did make a request for any pilots on-board to assist in landing. The volunteer was an off duty USAF B1-B captain. The fact remains that there are usually qualified airline, military, commercial cargo or even light aircraft pilots who fly on airliners as passengers; so the need for a completely unqualified civilian to be 'talked down' by air traffic controllers and/or qualified pilots, would be unheard of.
