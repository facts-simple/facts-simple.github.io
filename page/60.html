Large destruction, small death toll. The Great Fire of London.

2016-12-03

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Great Fire of London)
350 years ago on this date, the Great Fire of London had extinguished. According to Samuel Pepys, there were smaller fires still alight, but as he put it, 'the Great Fire was over'. The number of deaths is seen as inaccurate as these are the only verified deaths. Many who perished are thought to have been too poor or too low a class to have records made of them. The Great Fire of London did sweep past the walled boundary of the City, into the surrounding City of Westminster.

Exclusion of Pluto

2016-12-03

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (The Planets)
September is Classical Music Month. The Planets is a suite by the English composer Gustav Holst. It symbolises Astrology, so doesn't include Earth. The Suite is popular among film-makers and has appeared alongside many motion pictures on the Silver Screen. Clyde Tombaugh discovered Pluto in 1930. Which was then demoted to a dwarf planet in 2006 after the International Astronomical Union created the definition of a planet.

Including Bach would be &#39;showing off&#39;

2016-12-03

Fact Source(s):
September is Classical Music Month. The Voyager probes were launched in 1977 in hopes of studying Outer Space. Attached to both were Golden records, with instructions on how to read them, on which were images, sounds, music and greetings from Earth. The probes had basic information about humans and where Earth was in the Universe. Carl Sagan reportedly said that including Bach on the record would just be 'showing off'.
