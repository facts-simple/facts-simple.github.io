Nero didn&#39;t fiddle while Rome burned

2016-12-03

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Nero)
September is Classical Music Month. This is a good example of a prochronism (as opposed to an anachronism), in which an object appears in a time before its invention. ​ A more historically appropriate musical instrument would have been the lyre, similar in part to a harp. Tacitus, a historian of antiquity, states in his account of the time, that this was solely rumour: Nero did not play lyre and sing while Rome burned.

Beethoven: The Deaf Composer

2016-12-03

Fact Source(s):
  • Wikipedia (Ludwig van Beethoven)
September is Classical Music Month. Ludwig van Beethoven was undeterred by his hearing loss, but in 1811 the loss was so great that he was unable to conduct his Piano Concerto No 5 in Vienna. He conducted again in 1824 but had to be directed to face the applause as he could not hear it himself.
